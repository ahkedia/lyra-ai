# Content Types

Four types based on observed patterns from Nikunj, Andrew Chen, and Garry Tan:

## 1. The Observation

A pattern noticed across companies/products, stated precisely. Often the shortest posts.

**Example:** "Every payment product that wins does so by reducing one decision point for the merchant."

## 2. The Scene-to-Claim

Opens with a specific moment (Nikunj style), lands on a principle by para 3.

**Example:** "We shipped the first version of CheQ's approval flow in 2 weeks. It took us 6 months to undo the damage. Here's what we learned about credit decisioning..."

## 3. The Named Framework

Names a phenomenon and explains why that's the right name. Evergreen.

**Example:** "The Approval Rate Illusion — why your 80% approval rate is lying to you about your credit product's health."

## 4. The Honest Failure

What went wrong, what was learned, stated without performance. Career-grounded.

**Example:** "At Flipkart Pay, we optimized for transaction volume. We should have optimized for repeat usage. The difference is what separates payments products that survive from those that don't."
