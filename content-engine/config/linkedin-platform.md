# LinkedIn Platform Rules

Mirrors `x-platform.md` for the LinkedIn surface. LinkedIn earns its own register because the affordances are different: more space than X, more permission for personal narrative than the blog, less analytical scaffolding than either.

## Formats (pick AFTER writing begins)

### Argument post (150-280 words)

Direct claim opener. Two or three supporting beats, numbered when the structure is genuinely list-shaped. Conversational close: advice, the repeated named phrase, or honest acknowledgment of nuance with a footnoted aside.

Use when the take is analytical and needs scaffolding to land.

### Aphorism post (40-80 words)

One pattern observation. One supporting line. One punchy close on contrast or numerical reframe.

The closest LinkedIn ever gets to an X post. Use when the claim is sharp enough to stand alone but needs the personal register LinkedIn gives you.

### Scene post (120-220 words)

Hook line with emotional or geographic anchor ("The craziest thing happened in NYC..."). Chronological scene. Personal coda about what it felt like or what it meant.

Use when the story is the argument. No "and here's the lesson" pivot.

## Register

First person consistently. Geographic anchor when natural (Bangalore, Mumbai, Berlin, SF). Concession-then-pivot is a native LinkedIn move: "Yes X is true, AND Y is also true." Genuine acknowledgment of complexity, distinct from the banned rhetorical pair in voice-canon.md.

Personal markers throughout, including the analytical posts. "A founder I angel invested in" beats "founders often." "I keep walking around the city" beats "people are noticing."

## Punctuation latitude

Trailing ellipses (...), exclamation marks, and asterisk footnotes are native to LinkedIn when they read as speech. The em-dash cap from voice-canon.md still applies: zero em-dashes on LinkedIn.

Do not import this looseness back to the blog.

## Closing rule

The blog's 4-7 word instructional close from Rule 8 does not transfer. LinkedIn closes are personal beats.

**What works:**
- Soft echo of the named phrase
- A practical observation ("do your homework on the company you're joining")
- A personal beat ("I'm so excited to see where this goes")
- A footnoted nuance that complicates without undermining

**What to avoid:**
- Blog-style imperatives ("Play your own game.")
- Questions to the reader ("what do you think?")
- Motivational summaries ("the future belongs to...")

## Hard cap

280 words. Anything longer is the blog leaking in. If you've crossed 300, the piece is a blog post and should be written as one.

## Frequency

2-4 posts per week. Match the rhythm `x-platform.md` recommends. Every post worth reading.

## Rules that do not transfer from voice-canon.md

Rule 6 (organic parallelism only): LinkedIn allows numbered lists when the substance is genuinely list-shaped.

Rule 8 (instructional close): LinkedIn closes are personal. The 4-7 word imperative does not apply.

Everything else transfers. No preamble. First-person specifics. No "Not X, but Y" cadence. No em-dashes. Preserve idiosyncrasy.
