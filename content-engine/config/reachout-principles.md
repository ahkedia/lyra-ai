# Reachout Principles

For cold outbound to recruiters, hiring managers, founders, or operators you don't know. Private, one-to-one, short. The voice canon rules mostly transfer; the platform shape does not.

Three rules.

## 1. Open on something specific to them

A specific observation about their company that proves you've actually thought about it: a product decision, a hiring pattern, a recent shift, a number from their last announcement. Skip the flattery and the résumé recap.

If the opening sentence could be sent to ten other people without changing a word, rewrite it.

## 2. One sentence on what you've done that maps to their problem

One sentence on what you've done that maps to the problem you opened with. Background recap is not the move.

"I led credit risk at CheQ where we cut application abandonment 40%" lands. "I have 8 years of fintech experience" does not. The first is evidence. The second is résumé summary.

## 3. The ask is concrete, small, and seven words or fewer

"15 min next week?" beats "would love to chat."
"Open to coffee Tuesday?" beats "let me know if you'd ever want to connect."

The ask is what the reader acts on. If the ask is vague, the reply is none.

## Length cap

150 words. Three paragraphs, one per rule. If you can't say it in 150, you have not figured out what you want.

## What does not transfer from voice-canon.md

Rule 8 (instructional close) does not apply. The close is the ask.

Rule 2 (named thing as spine) does not apply at this length. Naming a phenomenon takes more room than a reachout has.

Everything else transfers: no preamble, first-person specifics, no em-dashes, no "Not X, but Y" cadence, preserve idiosyncrasy.
